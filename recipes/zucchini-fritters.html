Zucchini Fritter Shields

Description

These golden zucchini fritters have been a beloved staple in our hall for many summers — crisp-edged on the outside, soft at heart, and quick to make.

Served warm with cool sour cream, they’re tiny green shields of flavor, perfect for feeding your clan from the garden’s bounty.

Ingredients

  • 2 lb zucchini, about 2 large or 5 medium
  • 1 1/2 tsp fine sea salt, divided
  • 2 large eggs, lightly beaten
  • 1/2 cup chopped green onions
  • 3/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 1/2 tsp ground black pepper
  • olive oil for sautéing
  • sour cream, to serve

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Garden's Bounty

    Wash your zucchini harvest and trim off their ends.

    Grate them using the large holes of a box grater (or a food processor grater if you prefer swifter magic).

    Place the shredded zucchini in a large mixing bowl, stir in 1 tsp salt, and let them rest for 10 minutes—allowing the moisture to surrender before the battle begins.

  2. Drain the Waters of Midgard

    After their 10-minute rest, take the salted zucchini and squeeze them mightily over the sink to drive out the hidden waters. (If you wield a cheesecloth, wring them out like a seasoned warrior—far more liquid will fall than you expect!)

    You should be left with 3½ to 4 cups of dry, battle-ready zucchini.

    Return them to a large mixing bowl, prepared for the next stage of your fritter quest.

  3. Summon the Binders of the Realm

    To your battle-ready zucchini, add ½ cup chopped green onion for a spark of garden brightness, then stir in 2 lightly beaten eggs — the golden binders that will unite your fritter forces as one.

  4. Forge the Fritter Batter

    In a small bowl, whisk together ¾ cup flour, 1 tsp baking powder, ½ tsp salt, and ½ tsp black pepper (or to taste) — your dry runes of power.

    Fold this flour mixture into the zucchini blend, stirring until united in a smooth, battle-ready batter.

  5. Sear the Shields of Summer

    Set a heavy-bottomed cast iron or non-stick skillet over medium heat, and pour in 2 Tbsp olive oil, letting it shimmer like the forges of the gods.

    Scoop the zucchini mixture into the pan, a heaping tablespoon at a time, gently flattening each into a tiny battle shield.Sear 3–5 minutes per side until golden brown and crisp at the edges.

    If they brown too quickly, lower the flame to avoid scorching your warriors.Serve these shields of summer warm, crowned with a dollop of cool sour cream.
