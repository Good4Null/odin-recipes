Valhalla Zuppa

Description

Forged in the cauldrons of mortal kitchens yet worthy of the feasting halls of Valhalla, this hearty Zuppa Toscana brims with Italian sausage, crisp bacon, tender potatoes, and vibrant kale.

It’s a soul-warming soup, inspired by the legendary bowls of Olive Garden, reborn here as a comforting dish fit for the gods.

Ingredients

  • 6 oz bacon, chopped
  • 1 lb Italian Sausage, The "Hot" variety
  • 1 medium head garlic, 10 large cloves, peeled and minced or pressed
  • 1 medium onion, finely diced
  • 6 cups chicken broth/stock, (48 oz)
  • 4 cups water, (32 oz)
  • 5 medium russet potatoes, peeled and chopped into 1/4" thick pieces
  • 1 kale bundle, leaves stripped and chopped (6 cups)
  • 1 cup whipping cream
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Parmesan cheese to serve, optional

Instructions

  1. Awaken the Fires

    In a large 5.5-qt pot or Dutch oven, over medium-high heat, cook the chopped bacon until it turns crisp and golden (5–7 min).

    Transfer to a paper towel–lined plate, and carefully spoon out excess fat, leaving about 1 Tbsp behind to flavor the cauldron.

  2. Summon the Sausage

    Add the Italian sausage, breaking it apart with your spatula as it sears.

    Cook until browned through (about 5 min), then transfer to the plate of resting bacon warriors.

  3. Stir the Aromatics of Midgard

    Add the finely diced onion to the pot, sauté until soft and golden (about 5 min).

    Stir in minced garlic and cook 1 min until fragrant as a northern wind.

  4. Call the Broth to Boil

    Pour in 6 cups broth and 4 cups water, then bring to a rolling boil.

    Add the sliced potatoes and cook 13–14 min, until they yield easily to a fork.

  5. Unite the Warriors

    When the potatoes are nearly tender, stir in the chopped kale and cooked sausage, returning the pot to a gentle boil to meld the flavors.

  6. Cream of the Gods

    Pour in 1 cup cream, stirring as the soup returns to a soft boil.

    Season to taste with salt and black pepper, then remove from the heat.

  7. Bestow the Final Blessings

    Serve steaming hot, crowned with crispy bacon and grated parmesan, fit for the halls of Valhalla.
